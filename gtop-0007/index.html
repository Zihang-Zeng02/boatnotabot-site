Definition. Open Cover [gtop-0007]

  • March 8, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng

Let \((X,\tau )\) be a topological space and \(A \subseteq X\). An open cover of \(A\) is a collection \(\{U_\alpha \}_{\alpha \in I}\) of open sets such that \(A \subseteq \bigcup _{\alpha \in I}U_{\alpha }\).

A subcover is a subcollection \(\{U_\alpha \}_{\alpha \in J}\) with \(J \subseteq I\) that still covers \(A\). A subcover is called finite is \(J\) is a finite set.

    Definition. Topological Space [gtop-0002]

    • March 7, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A topological space is a pair \((X, \tau )\) where \(X\) is a set and \(\tau \subseteq \mathcal {P}(X)\) is a collection of subsets of \(X\), called a topology on \(X\), satisfying:

    1. \(\varnothing , X \in \tau \)
    2. If \(\left \{ U_\alpha \right \}_{\alpha \in A} \subseteq \tau \), then \(\bigcup _{\alpha \in A} U_\alpha \in \tau \) (closed under arbitrary unions).
    3. If \(U_1, U_2 \in \tau \), then \(U_1 \cap U_2 \in \tau \) (closed under finite intersections).

    The elements of \(\tau \) are called open sets. A set \(C \subseteq X\) is called closed if its complement \(X \setminus C\) is open.

    Example. Topologies on a Three-Point Set [gtop-000P]

    • March 11, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    Let \(X = \left \{ 1, 2, 3 \right \}\). The following are all valid topologies on \(X\):

    • Indiscrete: \(\tau _1 = \left \{ \varnothing , X \right \}\). Only \(\varnothing \) and \(X\) are open.
    • Partial: \(\tau _2 = \left \{ \varnothing , \left \{ 1 \right \}, X \right \}\). Only \(\left \{ 1 \right \}\) and the trivial sets are open.
    • Discrete: \(\tau _3 = \mathcal {P}(X)\). Every subset is open.

    Each satisfies the three axioms of a topological space. The same set \(X\) carries different topological structure depending on the choice of \(\tau \).

    Example. Open, Closed, Both, or Neither [gtop-000Q]

    • March 11, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    Let \(X = \left \{ 1,2,3 \right \}\) with \(\tau = \left \{ \varnothing , \left \{ 1 \right \}, X \right \}\). A subset need not be open or closed — it can be both, or neither.

    • \(\left \{ 1 \right \}\) is open (in \(\tau \)), but not closed (\(X \setminus \left \{ 1 \right \} = \left \{ 2,3 \right \} \notin \tau \)).
    • \(\left \{ 2,3 \right \}\) is closed (\(X \setminus \left \{ 2,3 \right \} = \left \{ 1 \right \} \in \tau \)), but not open.
    • \(\varnothing \) and \(X\) are both open and closed.
    • \(\left \{ 1,2 \right \}\) is neither open (not in \(\tau \)) nor closed (\(X \setminus \left \{ 1,2 \right \} = \left \{ 3 \right \} \notin \tau \)).

    Definition. Metric Space [gtop-0003]

    • March 7, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A metric space is a pair \((X, d)\) where \(X\) is a set and \(d : X \times X \to \mathbb {R}\) is a function called a metric, satisfying for all \(x, y, z \in X\):

    1. \(d(x, y) \geq 0\) and \(d(x, y) = 0\) if and only if \(x = y\) (positive definiteness).
    2. \(d(x, y) = d(y, x)\) (symmetry).
    3. \(d(x, z) \leq d(x, y) + d(y, z)\) (triangle inequality).

    Every metric space \((X, d)\) induces a topology on \(X\), called the metric topology, where the open sets are unions of open balls.

    Definition. Open Ball [gtop-000S]

    • March 11, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    Let \((X, d)\) be a metric space. The open ball centered at \(x \in X\) with radius \(\epsilon > 0\) is

    \[B(x, \epsilon ) = \left \{ y \in X : d(x, y) < \epsilon \right \}\]

    Notation. Metric Topology [gtop-000R]

    • March 11, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A metric space \((X, d)\) induces a topology \(\tau _d\) on \(X\) by declaring \(U \in \tau _d\) if and only if for every \(x \in U\) there exists \(\epsilon > 0\) such that the open ball \(B(x, \epsilon ) \subseteq U\).

    That is, a metric \(d\) induces a topology through the following chain:

    1. The metric \(d\) defines open balls \(B(x, \epsilon )\).
    2. Open balls generate open sets: a set is open if and only if it is an arbitrary union of open balls.
    3. The collection of all open sets forms \(\tau _d\), a valid topology.

    Every metric space is a topological space, but not every topological space arises from a metric. A topological space \((X, \tau )\) is called metrizable if there exists a metric \(d\) on \(X\) such that \(\tau = \tau _d\).

    Definition. Euclidean Space [gtop-0004]

    • March 7, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    The Euclidean space \(\mathbb {R}^n\) is the metric space equipped with the Euclidean metric

    \[d(x, y) = \lVert x - y \rVert = \sqrt {\sum _{i=1}^{n} (x_i - y_i)^2}\]

    The topology induced by this metric is called the standard topology on \(\mathbb {R}^n\). In this topology, the open ball centered at \(x\) with radius \(\epsilon > 0\) is

    \[B(x, \epsilon ) = \left \{ y \in \mathbb {R}^n : \lVert y - x \rVert < \epsilon \right \}\]

    This is a special case of a metric space, which in turn induces a topological space.

    Definition. Open Set [gtop-0005]

    • March 7, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    Let \((X, \tau )\) be a topological space. A subset \(U \subseteq X\) is called open if \(U \in \tau \).

    In a Euclidean space \(\mathbb {R}^n\), a subset \(U \subseteq \mathbb {R}^n\) is open if for every \(x \in U\), there exists \(\epsilon > 0\) such that \(B(x, \epsilon ) \subseteq U\). More generally, in any metric space \((X, d)\), a set is open if it is a union of open balls.

    The Euclidean definition is a special case where the topology is induced by the metric \(d(x, y) = \lVert x - y \rVert \).

    Definition. Closed Set [gtop-0006]

    • March 7, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    Let \((X, \tau )\) be a topological space. A subset \(C \subseteq X\) is called closed if its complement \(X \setminus C\) is open, i.e. \(X \setminus C \in \tau \).

    Equivalently, the collection of closed sets satisfies:

    1. \(\varnothing \) and \(X\) are closed.
    2. Arbitrary intersections of closed sets are closed.
    3. Finite unions of closed sets are closed.

    Theorem. Closed Set Characterization in Metric Spaces [gtop-000U]

    • March 11, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    Let \((X, d)\) be a metric space and \(C \subseteq X\). Then \(C\) is closed if and only if \(C\) contains all its limit points.

    Equivalently, \(C\) is closed if and only if for every convergent sequence \(\left \{ x_n \right \} \subseteq C\) with \(x_n \to x\), we have \(x \in C\).

    This characterization relies on the metric structure. In a general topological space, sequences may not suffice to detect all limit points.

    Definition. Compactness [gtop-0008]

    • March 7, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A topological space \((X, \tau )\) is compact if every open cover of \(X\) has a finite subcover.

    A subset \(K \subseteq X\) is compact if it is compact as a topological space with the subspace topology, or equivalently, if every open cover of \(K\) by open sets in \(X\) has a finite subcover.

    In Euclidean space \(\mathbb {R}^n\), the Heine-Borel theorem gives a concrete characterization: a subset \(K \subseteq \mathbb {R}^n\) is compact if and only if it is closed and bounded (i.e. \(K \subseteq B(0, R)\) for some \(R > 0\)).

    Definition. Hausdorff Space [gtop-000B]

    • March 11, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A topological space is Hausdorff if for every pair of distinct points therein there exist a neighbourhood of each point that are disjoint from each other.

    Definition. Borel Measure [gtop-000C]

    • March 11, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A Borel measure on a topological space \(X\) is a measure \(\mu : \mathcal {B}(X) \to [0, \infty ]\) defined on the Borel \(\sigma \)-algebra.

    Definition. Regularity [gtop-000G]

    • March 10, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    Regularity properties describe how well a Borel measure can be approximated by open or compact sets. A measure satisfying both outer and inner regularity is called regular.

    Definition. Outer Regularity [gtop-000D]

    • March 10, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A Borel measure \(\mu \) on a topological space \(X\) is outer regular on a Borel set \(E\) if \[ \mu (E) = \inf \left \{ \mu (W) : E \subseteq W, \; W \text { is open} \right \} \] where \(W\) is an open set.

    That is, the measure of \(E\) can be approximated from above by the measures of open sets containing \(E\).

    Definition. Inner Regularity [gtop-000E]

    • March 10, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A Borel measure \(\mu \) on a topological space \(X\) is inner regular on a Borel set \(E\) if \[ \mu (E) = \sup \left \{ \mu (M) : M \subseteq E, \; M \text { is compact} \right \} \] where \(M\) is a compact subset.

    That is, the measure of \(E\) can be approximated from below by the measures of compact subsets of \(E\).

    Definition. \(\sigma \)-algebra [gtop-000M]

    • March 10, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    A \(\sigma \)-algebra on a set \(X\) is a collection \(\mathcal {F} \subseteq \mathcal {P}(X)\) of subsets of \(X\) satisfying:

    1. \(X \in \mathcal {F}\).
    2. If \(A \in \mathcal {F}\), then \(X \setminus A \in \mathcal {F}\) (closed under complements).
    3. If \(\left \{ A_n \right \}_{n=1}^{\infty } \subseteq \mathcal {F}\), then \(\bigcup _{n=1}^{\infty } A_n \in \mathcal {F}\) (closed under countable unions).

    The pair \((X, \mathcal {F})\) is called a measurable space, and the members of \(\mathcal {F}\) are called measurable sets.
