1. Bolza Problem [opt-6002]

  • February 21, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng

Bolza problem is a fundamental formulation in the calculus of variations and optimal control that minimizes a cost functional consisting of both an integral term (running cost) and a terminal cost \[J := \int _0^{t_f} L \big (x(t), u(t)\big ) dt + \Phi \begin {pmatrix} x(t_f) \end {pmatrix}\] where \(L:\mathbb {R}^n \times \mathbb {R}^k \to \mathbb {R}\) is the running cost and \(\Phi :\mathbb {R}^n \to \mathbb {R}\) is the terminal cost. The goal is to find the optimal control \(u(t)\in \mathbb {R}^k\) that minimizes this cost functional subject to the system dynamics and boundary conditions.

1.1. First-order Variation of the Bolza Functional [opt-6003]

  • February 22, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng

To find the optimal control \(u\), we consider the first-order variation of the functional \(J\) with respect to \(u\). By augmenting the cost functional with system dynamics \(\dot x = f(x, u)\) using the costate \(p\), we define:

\[ \frac {\partial J}{\partial u} = \begin {pmatrix} \frac {\partial f}{\partial u} \end {pmatrix}^\intercal p + \begin {pmatrix} \frac {\partial L}{\partial u} \end {pmatrix}^\intercal \]

We obtain the stationarity condition \(\frac {\partial J}{\partial u} \equiv 0\).

1.2. Optimality Conditions for Bolza Problem [opt-6004]

  • February 22, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng

The necessary conditions for the optimality of $\(\mathbf {u}\)(t)$ are given by the following set of equations:

1.2.1. State Equation

  • February 22, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng
 \[ \dot x = f\begin {pmatrix} x, u \end {pmatrix}, \quad x(0) = x_0 \]

1.2.2. Costate Equation

  • February 22, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng
 \[ \dot p = -\begin {pmatrix} \frac {\partial f}{\partial x} \end {pmatrix}^\intercal p - \begin {pmatrix} \frac {\partial L}{\partial x} \end {pmatrix}^\intercal \]

1.2.3. Stationarity Condition

  • February 22, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng
 \[ \begin {pmatrix} \frac {\partial f}{\partial u} \end {pmatrix}^\intercal p + \begin {pmatrix} \frac {\partial L}{\partial u} \end {pmatrix}^\intercal = 0 \]

1.2.4. Boundary Condition (Transversality)

  • February 22, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng
 \[ p(t_f) = \nabla \Phi \begin {pmatrix} x(t_f) \end {pmatrix} \]
