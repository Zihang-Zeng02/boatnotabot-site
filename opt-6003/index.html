First-order Variation of the Bolza Functional [opt-6003]

  • February 22, 2026
  • Zihang Zeng

To find the optimal control \(\mathbf {u}\), we consider the first-order variation of the functional \(J\) with respect to \(\mathbf {u}\). By augmenting the cost functional with system dynamics \(\dot \mathbf {x} = f(\mathbf {x}, \mathbf {u})\) using the costate \(\mathbf {p}\), we define:

\[ \frac {\partial J}{\partial \mathbf {u}} = \left ( \frac {\partial f}{\partial \mathbf {u}} \right )^\intercal \mathbf {p} + \left ( \frac {\partial L}{\partial \mathbf {u}} \right )^\intercal \]

We obtain the stationarity condition \(\frac {\partial J}{\partial \mathbf {u}} \equiv 0\).

    Bolza Problem [opt-6002]

    • February 21, 2026
    • Zihang Zeng

    Bolza problem is a fundamental formulation in the calculus of variations and optimal control that minimizes a cost functional consisting of both an integral term (running cost) and a terminal cost \[J := \int _0^{t_f} L \big (\mathbf {x}(t), \mathbf {u}(t)\big ) dt + \Phi \left ( \mathbf {x}(t_f) \right )\] where state \(\mathbf {x}(t)\in \mathbb {R}^n\), control \(\mathbf {u}(t)\in \mathbb {R}^k\), \(L:\mathbb {R}^n \times \mathbb {R}^k \to \mathbb {R}\) is the running cost and \(\Phi :\mathbb {R}^n \to \mathbb {R}\) is the terminal cost. The goal is to find the optimal control \(\mathbf {u}^*(t)\in \mathbb {R}^k\) that minimizes this cost functional subject to the system dynamics and boundary conditions.
